Official synopsis

An utterly useless, pathetic F-Rank older brother who only drags down his successful S-Rank younger brother.

After living a messed-up life, eventually dragging even my brother’s life down with me, I was given the title after my regression:

‘Perfect Nurturer.’

Fine, this time, I’ll just stay quiet and support those brilliant guys from the shadows.

Or so I thought, but the S-Ranks are acting a little strange.

Main characters

Han Yoojin

Han Yoojin (Korean: 한유진, RR: Han Yujin) is an stat F-rank Hunter and the protagonist of The S-Ranks That I Raised. He dedicates his childhood to raising his younger brother Han Yoohyun. When Yoohyun eventually Awakens as an S-rank Hunter and later sacrifices himself to save his brother's life, Han Yoojin goes back in time to prevent his death.

Han Yoohyun

Han Yoohyun (Korean: 한유현, RR: Han Yuhyeon) is one of the strongest Korean S-rank Hunters and the leader of Haeyeon Guild. He is the younger brother of Han Yoojin, who he is a steadfast supporter of and for whom he sacrificed himself in the original timeline.

Bak Yerim

Bak Yerim (Korean: 박예림, RR: Bak Yerim) is an S-rank Hunter specializing in combat magic. She is an adoptive member of Han Yoojin's family and a member of Haeyeon Guild.

Sung Hyunjae

Sung Hyunjae (Korean: 성현제, RR: Seong Hyeonje) is the leader of Seseong Guild and one of the strongest Korean S-rank Hunters. He is an extremely talented and skilled man with a penchant for pursuing interesting or fun things, and holds a particular interest in Han Yoojin and Song Taewon.

Song Taewon

Song Taewon (Korean: 송태원, RR: Song Taewon) is an S-rank Hunter affiliated with the Hunter Association. He is the only S-rank Hunter to work under the nation rather than a guild[1], which is a subject of dissent between him and the other South Korean S-rank Hunters.

Peace

Peace (Korean: 피스, RR: Piseu) is a Horned Flame Lion, a Tier 2 fire-attribute monster, who was raised by Han Yoojin. He currently acts as the kiseungsu for Haeyeon Guild Leader Han Yoohyun; when not accompanying him on dungeon raids, Peace stays with Han Yoojin at the kiseungsu rearing facility.

Chirpie

Chirpie (Korean: 삐약, RR: Ppiyak, lit. "chirp") is a mutant monster that was found by and currently resides with Han Yoojin. It is slightly glitched, not appearing properly in the system. Chirpie's rank and abilities are shrouded in mystery, with it temporarily revealing skills as high as L-rank.

Personal review

Many will call this a copy of Solo Leveling, however the moment you give it a try you notice that it couldn't be further from the truth.

I love this novel and has become one of my all time favorites. At the beginning it's a bit slow and some plot points feel disjointed (this gets better further on, as the author gains experience); however the character building never misses. Geunso is stellar when it comes to explore the characters and their relationships.

Unlike many stories (novels and manhwas alike), this one lacks a romantic subplot. Yet, in my eyes, it's the one that delves deeper into themes of love and all the forms it could take besides the romantic love media has us all used to. From the love shown between brothers, to the one shown between a parent towards their kids, to the bond shared between kindred souls. The nuance and layers each relationship shows and how it changes through chapters it's poetic and beautiful to read.

My favorite part of the novel is how the author portrays Han Yoojin's mental state through narration. We meet the protagonist at a deeply unstable point in his life, saying he was at rock bottom wouldn't feel like an exaggeration and most of the novel is seen through his rather askew point of view, his impulses, self-loathing and normalization of pain lead us through this magnificent world of monsters and hunters. Yoojin is one of the best examples of an unreliable narrator, with his true colors and emotions flowing slowly through cracks requiring the audience to read between lines and uncover the lies he tells to himself for his own peace of mind. I find it a pity how the manhwa failed to show the most crocked sides of this character in favor of comedy and a lower PG-rating.

I could spend numerous paragraphs talking about the novel and it's writing, however this review would turn into a retelling or essay instead. I would recommend anyone to read this novel more so if you enjoy character driven stories with complex characters and relationships left and right.
